VOLLEYBALL

INTRODUCTION TO VOLLEYBALL:

~ Volleyball is a popular team sport where two teams of six players hit a ball over a high net, aiming to ground it on the opponent's court, using up to three hits per side. Invented in the U.S. in 1895, it's a dynamic game emphasizing teamwork, athleticism, and skill, involving serves, passes, sets, and spikes, played in best-of-five sets, with variations like beach and sitting volleyball.

GAMEPLAY:

Objective: Score points by making the ball land in the opponent's court or forcing them into an error.

Teams: Six players per side (indoor), with rotations after each serve.

Hits: Each team gets a maximum of three touches (hits) to return the ball.

Scoring: Points are scored when the ball hits the floor on the opponent's side or if the opponent faults. Matches are typically best-of-five sets (first to 25 points, win by two).

KEY SKILLS & ACTIONS:

Serve: The initial hit to start a rally, from behind the end line.

Pass:Using forearms to control and direct the ball after the serve or attack.

Set: A soft, overhead hit, usually by hands, to position the ball for an attacker.

Spike/Attack: A powerful, downward hit over the net.
